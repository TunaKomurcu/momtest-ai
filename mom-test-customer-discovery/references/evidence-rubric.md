# Mom Test Evidence Rubric

## Strong evidence

Count as strong evidence when the participant gives concrete proof such as:

- a recent specific example
- repeated occurrence
- named tools or people in the workflow
- a workaround they currently maintain
- money already spent
- time regularly spent
- reputation or operational risk
- active search for alternatives
- introduction to another stakeholder
- pilot, preorder, deposit, or scheduled next step

## Medium evidence

Count as medium evidence when the participant describes a plausible problem but lacks proof of urgency, cost, workaround, or commitment.

## Weak evidence

Count as weak evidence when the participant gives:

- praise
- opinions
- hypotheticals
- feature suggestions
- future-tense promises
- unsupported willingness to pay
- generic claims such as usually, always, or never

## Negative evidence

Count as negative evidence when:

- they cannot remember a recent example
- they do not currently solve the problem
- the problem has no meaningful cost
- the workaround is good enough
- they are not the buyer or user
- they are unreachable as a segment
- they resist any concrete next step
